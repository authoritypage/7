Local News: Government Issues in Santa Barbara County (2024-2025)

Key Points

  • Research suggests significant government mismanagement in Santa Barbara County, including fund misuse and decision-making issues.
  • Ambulance contract disputes and homelessness spending investigations reveal inconsistencies and potential fraud.
  • Recent Grand Jury reports highlight ongoing challenges in agency transparency and accountability.
  • A Santa Barbara County judge resigned amid severe ethics violations, underscoring judicial misconduct.
  • Ongoing federal litigation continues to highlight deficiencies in jail conditions, particularly for those with disabilities and mental health needs.

Additional Articles & Headlines (2024-2025)

  • Grand Jury Reports Highlight Agency Accountability Shortfalls: The 2024-2025 Santa Barbara County Grand Jury released consolidated responses to previous reports, indicating that local agencies agreed with approximately two-thirds of findings but faced challenges in implementing recommendations, particularly regarding the Lompoc Tourism Improvement District's finances and potential conflicts of interest in death-in-custody investigations. ([KEYT](https://keyt.com/news/california/2025/03/10/county-oversight-group-releases-round-up-of-previous-sessions-reports-and-agency-responses/), [edhat](https://www.edhat.com/news/santa-barbara-county-grand-jury-reports-local-agencies-agreed-to-nearly-two-thirds-of-all-findings/)) - *March 2025*
  • Santa Barbara Judge Resigns Amid Ethics Scandal: Judge Michael J. Carrozzo resigned and received a severe public censure and permanent ban from serving as a judicial officer in California after admitting to multiple acts of willful misconduct, including misrepresenting himself as an attorney to practice law while on the bench. ([KCBX](https://www.kcbx.org/central-coast-news/2025-04-25/santa-barbara-county-judge-to-resign-after-ethics-violations), [Santa Barbara Independent](https://www.independent.com/2025/04/22/santa-barbara-judge-agrees-to-lifetime-ban-from-bench/)) - *April 2025*
  • Federal Lawsuit Continues Over Santa Barbara County Jail Conditions: The "Murray v. County of Santa Barbara" class-action lawsuit continues to monitor the County's compliance with a stipulated judgment aimed at improving medical care, mental health services, disability accommodations, and reducing solitary confinement at the jail, with ongoing deficiencies noted. ([Disability Rights California - December 2024 Update](https://www.disabilityrightsca.org/system/files/file-attachments/2024-Dec%20Murray%20v.%20Cty%20of%20Santa%20Barbara%20Case%20Update_0.pdf), [The Santa Barbara Independent](https://www.independent.com/2025/03/27/too-many-unanswered-questions-about-jail-expansion/)) - *Ongoing, Latest Update December 2024 / March 2025 mentions*
